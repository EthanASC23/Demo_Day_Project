Rising sea levels that threaten to swallow entire coastlines and drowning cities are the result of global warming

As the planet warms, so does its oceans, warm water takes up more space than cold water, causing the oceans to expand and thus endangering coastline cities.

Global warming can lead to an increase in the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, such as hurricanes, typhoons, and heavy rainfall this endangers many places near a body of water

The icebergs in Antarctica are melting as a result of global warming. The ice acts as a habitat for wildlife in Antarctica. Without their habitats, the wildlife will intrude into other habitats or even being an extinct species

Hot countries often experience water scarcity due to high evaporation rates and limited freshwater resources. Global warming can worsen this situation by accelerating evaporation, reducing rainfall in some regions, and causing changes in precipitation patterns. This can lead to droughts, crop failures, and challenges in securing clean drinking water.
